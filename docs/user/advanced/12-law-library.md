# Law Library

The Law Library is a searchable repository of legislation, court rulings, legal principles, and your firm's own internal documents. It acts as the authoritative reference base for your firm's legal research and feeds the AI Research assistant with verified source material.

---

## What the Library Contains

| Document Type | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Legislation | Laws, codes, decrees, and regulations |
| Court Rulings | Published judgments from Egyptian and regional courts |
| Legal Principles | Established legal maxims and doctrines |
| Firm Documents | Your firm's internal precedents and custom materials |

**System documents** are provided and maintained by ELMS (legislation, published rulings). **Firm documents** are uploaded by your firm's administrator and are visible only to your firm.

---

## Browsing the Library

1. Click **Library** in the left sidebar.
2. The category tree on the left displays the hierarchy of legal domains (for example: Civil Law → Contracts → Sales).
3. Click any category to expand it and see its sub-categories.
4. Select a category to view the list of documents it contains on the right.
5. Click any document title to open the full-text viewer.

> [!TIP]
> If you are not sure which category to look in, use the Search function instead of browsing the tree.

---

## Viewing a Document

1. Click the document title from any list or search result.
2. The full-text viewer opens.
3. For legislation, the left panel shows a numbered article list. Click any article number to jump directly to that article.
4. Use the scroll bar or the article navigation panel to move through long documents.

---

## Searching the Library

1. Click **Library** in the sidebar, then click **Search** at the top of the library area.
2. Type your keywords in Arabic or English into the search box.
3. Press **Enter** or click the **Search** button.
4. Results show matching documents and the relevant passages where your keywords appear.
5. Click any result to open the full document at the matching location.

> [!TIP]
> Search automatically normalises Arabic text, so you do not need to worry about diacritics (tashkeel) or alef variants. The results will include all matching forms.

## Adding Annotations

Annotations let you attach private notes to any passage in a library document. Annotations are visible only to users within your firm.

1. Open any library document in the viewer.
2. Select (highlight) the text passage you want to annotate.
3. Click **Add Annotation** in the toolbar that appears.
4. Type your note in the annotation panel on the right.
5. Click **Save**.

To view existing annotations on a document, open the document — annotated passages are highlighted. Click a highlighted passage to read the note.

> [!NOTE]
> Annotations do not modify the original document. They are stored separately and are only visible to members of your firm.

---

## Linking a Library Document to a Case

Connecting a library document to a case keeps all your legal references organised in one place.

1. Open the case you are working on.
2. Click the **Legal References** tab inside the case.
3. Click **Add Reference**.
4. In the search box, type a keyword from the document title or article you want to link.
5. Select the document (or a specific article within that document) from the results.
6. Optionally, enter a note explaining the relevance of this reference to the case.
7. Click **Save**.

The reference now appears in the **Legal References** tab of the case and can be clicked to open the source document.

---

## Tagging Documents

Tags help organise documents and make filtering faster.

> [!NOTE]
> Only users with the **Firm Admin** role or equivalent library admin permissions can add or manage tags.

1. Open the document in the library viewer.
2. Click **Edit Tags** in the document details panel.
3. Type the tag name and press **Enter** to add it.
4. Click **Save**.

When browsing the library, use the **Filter by Tag** option to narrow results.

---

## Adding Firm Documents (Administrators)

Firm administrators can upload internal documents (precedents, templates, custom reference material) to the library.

1. Go to **Library** → **Admin** (visible only to administrators).
2. Click **Upload Document**.
3. Select the file (PDF or Word document).
4. Fill in the document details: title, type, category, and any tags.
5. Click **Save**.

The document is processed and becomes searchable within a few minutes. It is visible only to members of your firm.

> [!NOTE]
> Uploaded firm documents are subject to the same 50 MB file size limit as case documents. For very large documents, consider splitting them into sections.
